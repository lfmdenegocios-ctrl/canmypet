🐶 Dairy & eggs · vet-sourced verdicts

Can dogs have dairy and eggs?

Most adult dogs digest lactose poorly, so dairy is where good intentions meet upset stomachs. Cooked eggs, on the other hand, are one of the best cheap treats there is. The full dairy shelf, sorted by verdict.

✅ Safe in moderation (1)

🥚Cooked eggFully cooked eggs are a great source of protein for dogs. Avoid raw egg whites.Yes

⚠️ Caution — limits apply (7)

🧀CheeseMany dogs love cheese, but it’s high in fat and many dogs are lactose intolerant.In moderation🥛MilkCow’s milk often causes diarrhea and gas because most adult dogs don’t digest lactose well.In moderation🥣Plain yogurtPlain, unsweetened yogurt can be a probiotic treat, but watch for lactose sensitivity and added sugar.In moderation🧈ButterButter is not toxic but is pure fat and dairy, which can cause stomach upset or pancreatitis.In moderation🍦Ice creamIce cream is high in sugar and many dogs are lactose intolerant. Never give chocolate or sugar-free (xylitol) flavours.In moderation⚪Whipped creamA tiny lick of plain whipped cream is usually tolerated, but it is sugary, fatty and many dogs are lactose intolerant.In moderation🥚Raw eggSome dogs tolerate raw egg, but it carries a Salmonella risk, and raw egg whites contain avidin, which blocks a B-vitamin if fed often.
