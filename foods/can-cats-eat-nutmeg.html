Can cats eat nutmeg?

No — cats should never eat nutmeg.

Nutmeg contains myristicin, which is toxic to cats. Keep all nutmeg-spiced foods away.

Why this verdict for cats

Nutmeg contains myristicin, which is toxic to cats. Keep all nutmeg-spiced foods away.

Warning signs to watch for

  • Disorientation
  • Rapid heart rate
  • Tremors

⚠ If your cat ate nutmeg

Act immediately — don't wait for symptoms.

  • Call your vet or a pet poison helpline right away.
  • Note how much was eaten and when.
  • Do not induce vomiting unless a professional tells you to.

Nutmeg is a common baking spice that's harmless to us in food amounts but contains myristicin, which is toxic to dogs and cats.

How much is safe?

A tiny trace in a baked good is usually low-risk; the concern is larger amounts — straight nutmeg, eggnog and pumpkin-spice mixes. The risk rises around the holidays, so keep spiced treats out of reach.

What happens if a cat eats too much?

Signs can include disorientation, a racing heart, high blood pressure, belly pain, tremors and, in large amounts, seizures — and they can last a day or two.

⚠ What to do if your cat ate nutmeg

Call your vet or a poison line (ASPCA 888-426-4435 · Pet Poison Helpline 855-764-7661), especially if your pet got into straight nutmeg or eggnog. (Cinnamon, by contrast, isn't truly toxic — just irritating in large amounts.)

Frequently asked questions

Is a pinch of nutmeg in a treat dangerous?

A tiny trace is usually low-risk, but it's safest to skip nutmeg-spiced foods entirely.

What about pumpkin pie or eggnog?

Both often contain nutmeg (and eggnog may have alcohol) — keep them away from pets.
