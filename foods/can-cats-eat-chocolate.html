Can cats eat chocolate?

No — cats should never eat chocolate.

Cats are also highly sensitive to theobromine and caffeine in chocolate. Any amount should be treated as an emergency.

Why this verdict for cats

Cats are also highly sensitive to theobromine and caffeine in chocolate. Any amount should be treated as an emergency.

Warning signs to watch for

  • Vomiting
  • Rapid breathing
  • Tremors

⚠ If your cat ate chocolate

Act immediately — don't wait for symptoms.

  • Call your vet or a pet poison helpline right away.
  • Note how much was eaten and when.
  • Do not induce vomiting unless a professional tells you to.

Chocolate is the classic dog poison for good reason: it contains theobromine and caffeine, which dogs clear very slowly, letting them build to toxic levels.

How much is safe?

Danger depends on the type and your dog's size. Baking and dark chocolate are the most dangerous, milk chocolate less so, and white chocolate has almost no theobromine. As a rough guide, signs can begin around 20 mg of theobromine per kg of body weight — that can be just a few squares of dark chocolate for a small dog. Use our chocolate calculator to estimate the risk.

What happens if a cat eats too much?

Signs usually show within 6–12 hours: vomiting and diarrhea, restlessness, a racing heart, high body temperature, tremors and, in severe cases, seizures.

⚠ What to do if your cat ate chocolate

Call your vet or a poison line right away (ASPCA 888-426-4435 · Pet Poison Helpline 855-764-7661). Have your dog's weight, the type of chocolate, and the amount and time ready. If it was very recent, the vet may induce vomiting.

Frequently asked questions

How much chocolate is toxic to a dog?

It depends on type and weight — dark and baking chocolate are dangerous in small amounts, milk chocolate less so. Our chocolate calculator gives an estimate.

My dog ate chocolate but seems fine — should I worry?

Signs can take hours to appear, so don't wait. Call a poison line with the details for tailored advice.

Is white chocolate dangerous?

It has very little theobromine, so toxicity risk is low — but it's very fatty and sugary, which can still upset the stomach.
