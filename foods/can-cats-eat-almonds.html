Cats can eat almonds, but only in moderation.

Why this verdict for cats

Almonds aren’t a natural food for cats and pose a choking risk. Better skipped.

Almonds aren't toxic the way macadamias are, but they're still a poor snack for dogs — and not something cats should have.

How much is safe?

A single dropped almond probably won't cause harm, but they're hard to digest, high in fat, and a choking or obstruction risk for small dogs. Flavoured or salted almonds are worse.

What happens if a cat eats too much?

Several almonds can cause an upset stomach, vomiting or diarrhea from the fat; whole nuts can choke or, rarely, block the gut — and salted ones add a sodium load.

How to serve it safely

Honestly, almonds are best skipped — there are far better treats (carrot, blueberries, plain chicken). If your dog grabs one, watch for choking or stomach upset.

Frequently asked questions

Are almonds toxic to dogs?

Not toxic like macadamias, but the fat and choking risk make them a treat to avoid.

What about almond butter or almond milk?

Plain, xylitol-free versions in tiny amounts are low-risk, but they're fatty and unnecessary.
