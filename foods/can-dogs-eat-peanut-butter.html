Can dogs eat peanut butter?

Dogs can eat peanut butter, but only in moderation.

Why this verdict for dogs

Plain peanut butter is a favorite treat — but always check the label: any with xylitol is deadly.

Plain peanut butter is a classic dog treat — great on a lick mat or for hiding pills — with one critical catch: it must be xylitol-free.

How much is safe?

Always read the label first. A few 'sugar-free' peanut butters use xylitol, which is deadly to dogs even in tiny amounts. Stick to plain PB whose only ingredients are peanuts (maybe salt), in small amounts because it's high in fat.

What happens if a dog eats too much?

Plain PB in excess just means too much fat and calories — possible upset stomach or weight gain. The real danger is xylitol, which causes a sudden blood-sugar crash and liver damage.

How to serve it safely

Smear a thin layer on a lick mat or inside a rubber toy to keep your dog busy, or use a dab to give pills. Choose unsalted, xylitol-free brands; natural PB (just peanuts) is ideal.

Frequently asked questions

Which peanut butters contain xylitol?

A handful of 'sugar-free' brands — always check the ingredient list (it may be listed as 'birch sugar') before sharing.

How much peanut butter can my dog have?

A small spoonful for a big dog, less for a small one — it's a treat, kept within the 10% rule.

Can cats have peanut butter?

It's not toxic if xylitol-free, but it's fatty and offers cats little — a rare tiny lick at most.
