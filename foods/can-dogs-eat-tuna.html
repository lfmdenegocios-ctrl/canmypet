Can dogs eat tuna?

Dogs can eat tuna, but only in moderation.

A little plain tuna is fine, but it’s high in mercury and salt and shouldn’t be a regular food.

Why this verdict for dogs

A little plain tuna is fine, but it’s high in mercury and salt and shouldn’t be a regular food.

A little cooked or water-packed canned tuna is a fine occasional treat — and famously beloved by cats. The problem is the habit: tuna every day is where the risks live.

How much is safe?

A small spoonful of plain tuna canned in water (not oil, not salted) or a bite of cooked fresh tuna, occasionally. For cats especially, tuna should be a treat — not a meal replacement.

What happens if a dog eats too much?

Tuna is higher in mercury than smaller fish, so a steady tuna habit builds exposure over time. In cats, a tuna-heavy diet is also nutritionally unbalanced and can cause health problems — and some cats get so hooked they refuse normal food.

How to serve it safely

Offer plain tuna in water, drained, as an occasional treat or food topper. Skip tuna in oil, salted tuna, and anything seasoned.

Frequently asked questions

Can cats eat tuna every day?

No — daily tuna risks mercury build-up and an unbalanced diet, and can turn cats into stubborn tuna-only eaters. Keep it occasional.

Is canned tuna in oil ok?

Water-packed is the right choice — oil adds unnecessary fat, and salted versions add sodium.

Can pets eat raw tuna (sushi-grade)?

Best not — raw fish carries parasite risk and an enzyme that breaks down vitamin B1. Cooked is safer.
