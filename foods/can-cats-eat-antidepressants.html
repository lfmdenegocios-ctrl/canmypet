Can cats eat antidepressants?

No — cats should never eat antidepressants.

Human antidepressants are dangerous to cats and can cause tremors, a racing heart and agitation. Treat ingestion as an emergency.

🚨 Keep this away. If your cat ate some, don't wait — call your vet or poison control (ASPCA 888-426-4435).

Why this verdict for cats

Human antidepressants are dangerous to cats and can cause tremors, a racing heart and agitation. Treat ingestion as an emergency.

Warning signs to watch for

  • Agitation
  • Tremors
  • Dilated pupils

⚠ If your cat ate antidepressants

Act immediately — don't wait for symptoms.

  • Call your vet or a pet poison helpline right away.
  • Note how much was eaten and when.
  • Do not induce vomiting unless a professional tells you to.
