Can dogs eat strawberries?

Yes — dogs can safely eat strawberries.

Strawberries are safe and full of vitamin C and fiber, but sugary — keep portions small.

Why this verdict for dogs

Strawberries are safe and full of vitamin C and fiber, but sugary — keep portions small.

Strawberries are a safe, sweet treat full of vitamin C, fiber and antioxidants — just remember they're sugary, so portions stay small.

How much is safe?

One or two sliced strawberries make a nice treat for most dogs. Because of the natural sugar, keep them occasional — especially for small or diabetic dogs.

What happens if a dog eats too much?

Too many can cause a sugary upset stomach or loose stool; canned or syrup strawberries add way too much sugar (and possibly xylitol).

How to serve it safely

Wash and slice fresh strawberries (remove the leafy top); serve fresh, mashed or frozen. Avoid canned, sugared or chocolate-covered versions.

Frequently asked questions

Can dogs eat strawberry tops/leaves?

The green tops aren't toxic but are tough and best removed to avoid choking.

How many strawberries can my dog have?

One or two for a small dog, a few for a large one — a treat kept within the 10% rule.
