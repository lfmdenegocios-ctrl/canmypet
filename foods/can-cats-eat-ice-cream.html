Can cats eat ice cream?

Cats can eat ice cream, but only in moderation.

Most cats are lactose intolerant, so ice cream commonly causes stomach upset, and the sugar is unhealthy.

Why this verdict for cats

Most cats are lactose intolerant, so ice cream commonly causes stomach upset, and the sugar is unhealthy.

On a hot day a lick of ice cream seems harmless, but between the lactose, sugar and risky flavours, it's a treat to be careful with.

How much is safe?

A small lick of plain vanilla may be tolerated, but dairy upsets lactose-intolerant pets and the sugar isn't good for them. Crucially, never give chocolate ice cream or anything 'sugar-free' (it may contain xylitol, deadly to dogs).

What happens if a cat eats too much?

Expect possible diarrhea, gas and an upset stomach from the lactose and sugar; chocolate or xylitol flavours are an emergency.

How to serve it safely

Better swaps: a spoon of plain xylitol-free yogurt, 'nice cream' made from frozen mashed banana, or just frozen watermelon chunks — cool treats without the dairy and sugar.

Frequently asked questions

Can dogs have vanilla ice cream?

A small lick is usually just an upset-stomach risk, but a frozen banana or dog-safe alternative is a better idea.

Why is sugar-free ice cream dangerous?

It may contain xylitol, toxic to dogs even in small amounts — always avoid sugar-free desserts.
