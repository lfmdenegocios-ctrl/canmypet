How much should I feed my dog?

This estimates roughly how much food to give per day — turning calorie needs into actual cups or grams.

What the terms mean

  • kcal/cup — the calorie density printed on your food bag; the tool uses it to convert calories into a portion.
  • How it differs from the calorie tool — the calorie tool gives the energy target; this one turns that target into how much to scoop.

How to use it

Check your food's kcal/cup on the bag for the most accurate result, split the daily amount into two meals, and fine-tune by watching your dog's weight.

🩺 Vet note: Portion needs vary by metabolism and health — confirm for puppies, weight-loss plans or medical diets.

Common questions

Why does the bag's guide differ from this?

Bag guides are generous averages; this tailors to your pet's weight and goal — use the weight trend to fine-tune.

Cups or grams — which is better?

Weighing in grams is most accurate; cups are convenient but vary with scooping.

Once or twice a day?

Most adult dogs do best with two meals; puppies need more frequent ones.

How the calculation works

The calculator uses the standard veterinary energy formula. First it finds your dog's Resting Energy Requirement (RER) — the calories needed at rest — then multiplies it by an activity factor to get the daily energy need:

  • RER = 70 × (body weight in kg)0.75
  • Daily calories = RER × activity factor
  • Food grams = daily calories ÷ food energy density

Because every dog and every food is different, treat the result as a starting point. Monitor your dog's body condition and adjust over a few weeks.
