How many calories does my pet need?

This estimates how many calories your dog or cat needs each day, based on weight, life stage and goal.

What the numbers mean

  • Daily kcal — the total energy your pet needs per day.
  • Resting need (RER) — the calories just to rest — 70 × (weight in kg) to the power 0.75.
  • MER (maintenance) — RER × an activity/life-stage factor = the real daily target.
  • Treat budget — keep treats to about 10% of daily calories so they don't unbalance the diet.

How to use it

  1. Find your food's calories — check the bag for kcal per cup or per gram.
  2. Divide your pet's daily calorie target by that number to get the daily amount.
  3. Split it into two meals a day (more often for puppies).
  4. Keep treats to about 10% of the daily calories, and adjust by watching your pet's weight over a few weeks.
🩺 Vet note: Calorie needs vary with metabolism, neutering and health — confirm for puppies, weight-loss plans or medical diets with your vet.

Common questions

Is kcal the same as calories?

Yes — what we casually call a food calorie is technically a kilocalorie (kcal). They're the same here.

Two dogs weigh the same but need different amounts — why?

Activity, age, neutering and metabolism all change needs, which is why the tool asks more than just weight.

Should I feed the resting (RER) number?

No — RER is just the at-rest baseline. Feed the higher daily (MER) target unless your vet says otherwise.
