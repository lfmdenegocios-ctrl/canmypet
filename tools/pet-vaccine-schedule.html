Non-core vaccines (Lepto, Bordetella, Lyme, FeLV) depend on risk. Always confirm the plan with your veterinarian.

Understanding the vaccine schedule

This shows a typical core vaccination timeline for puppies/kittens and adults, that you can print.

What the terms mean

  • Core vs non-core — core vaccines are recommended for nearly all pets (e.g. rabies and distemper/parvo for dogs; rabies and panleukopenia for cats). Non-core depend on lifestyle and risk (e.g. Lyme, kennel cough, FeLV).
  • Booster — a repeat dose that keeps immunity strong.

How to use it

Use it as a general guide to discuss with your vet — exact timing and which non-core vaccines your pet needs depend on age, lifestyle and where you live.

🩺 Vet note: Always follow your vet's schedule and local laws (rabies is legally required in many places). This is educational, not a medical schedule.

Common questions

When do puppies start vaccines?

Usually around 6–8 weeks, with boosters every few weeks until about 16 weeks.

Does my indoor cat need vaccines?

Core vaccines (and often rabies by law) are still recommended; your vet advises on the rest.

What's a core vaccine?

One recommended for essentially all pets because the disease is severe and widespread.
