How much water should my pet drink?

A quick, weight-based estimate of healthy daily water intake — so you know when your dog or cat is drinking too little or too much.

A healthy dog drinks roughly 50–60 ml per kg of body weight per day; cats around 45–55 ml/kg (a lot of which can come from wet food). This is a guideline, not a rule — activity, heat, diet and health change it. A sudden, lasting increase or drop in thirst can signal a health issue, so mention big changes to your vet.

How much water does my pet need?

This estimates roughly how much water your dog or cat should drink in a day to stay properly hydrated.

What the numbers mean

  • ml/day — the total daily water target for your pet's size.
  • The rule behind it — vets estimate about 50–60 ml of water per kg of body weight for dogs, and 45–55 ml/kg for cats. The tool does that math for you.
  • Food counts too — wet food is mostly water, so pets on wet diets drink less from the bowl. That's normal, not a problem.

How to use it

Keep fresh water available at all times — the number is a target, not a limit. What matters most is a steady habit; watch for drinking a lot more or a lot less than usual.

🩺 Vet note: A sudden big change in thirst (much more or much less) can signal health issues like kidney disease or diabetes — worth a vet visit.

Common questions

My dog drinks less than the number — is that bad?

On wet food or in cool weather it's often fine. Watch for lethargy or signs of dehydration.

How do I check for dehydration?

Gently lift the skin between the shoulders (it should snap back) and check that gums are moist. Skin that tents, or dry tacky gums, mean call a vet.

My cat barely drinks — is that normal?

Cats evolved to get water from prey, so wet food helps. Very low intake on dry food is worth watching.
