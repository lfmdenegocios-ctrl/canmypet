What does a pet really cost?

This estimates what a dog or cat really costs — monthly, yearly, and over a lifetime.

What the terms mean

  • Recurring vs one-time — food, vet care and insurance repeat; adoption, spay/neuter and starter supplies are one-offs.
  • Lifetime cost — yearly cost × expected lifespan, plus those upfront one-time costs.

How to use it

Edit the pre-filled numbers to match your situation; use it to budget before adopting, or to plan savings and insurance.

💡 Heads-up: Unexpected vet bills are the biggest variable — an emergency fund or pet insurance softens the blow.

Common questions

Why is the lifetime number so big?

Small monthly costs add up over 10–15+ years — that's exactly what this makes visible.

Is pet insurance worth it?

It trades a monthly cost for protection against big surprise bills; it depends on your savings and risk tolerance.

Are these US averages?

Yes, rough ones — costs vary a lot by location, breed and choices, so edit them.
