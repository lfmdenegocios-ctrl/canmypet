Is it an emergency?

This helps you quickly judge whether something your pet ate needs urgent action, and gives you the poison-control numbers.

What to know

  • Poison-control lines — ASPCA Animal Poison Control 888-426-4435 and Pet Poison Helpline 855-764-7661, staffed by experts 24/7 (a consultation fee may apply).
  • Don't induce vomiting unless told to — for some substances, making a pet vomit makes things worse; only do it on a vet's or poison line's instruction.

How to use it

In a real emergency, call a vet or poison line first. Have your pet's weight, exactly what and how much they ate, and the time it happened ready.

🩺 Vet note: This is guidance, not a diagnosis — when in doubt, call. With true toxins, minutes matter.

Common questions

Should I make my dog throw up?

Only if a vet or poison line tells you to — for some substances it's harmful.

Is there a fee for poison control?

There can be a consultation fee, but it's usually worth it for fast expert advice.

What info should I have ready?

Your pet's weight, exactly what and how much was eaten, and when.
