Churrasco With Your Pet: What's Safe at a Brazilian BBQ?

🇧🇷🐶🍖⚽Brazil's on — and the churrasqueira is lit. Those big hopeful eyes by the grill deserve an honest answer: here's exactly what can and can't come off the skewer.

A Brazilian churrasco is a pet's dream: hours of sizzling meat at nose height. The good news — plain grilled beef is genuinely one of the safer human foods to share. The bad news — almost everything around it (the seasoning, the bones, the vinaigrette, the sausage, the farofa) is where the trouble lives. Here's the skewer-by-skewer breakdown.

⚠️ If your pet grabs a bone, linguiça or anything garlicky — act, don't wait. Call your vet or a poison line: ASPCA 888-426-4435 · Pet Poison Helpline 855-764-7661. Onion and garlic signs can take a day or more to show. Our emergency check takes 60 seconds.

✅ Safe to share (small and plain)

Plain grilled beef Safe

A small piece of well-cooked, unseasoned beef — cut from the center, away from the salt crust and the fat cap — is a lean, protein-rich treat most dogs and cats love.

Serve: a bite-size piece or two, no seasoning, no fat, no bone. Treats stay within ~10% of daily calories.

Watermelon (the dessert table) Safe

The classic churrasco dessert is also the classic pet-safe treat — about 92% water. Seedless chunks of the red flesh only.

Serve: bite-size seedless cubes; skip the rind.

🚫 The churrasco classics that stay on the human plate

Bones & skewers Never

The #1 churrasco emergency. Cooked bones splinter into shards, and wooden skewers smell like picanha but can pierce the gut. Secure the discard pile — dogs raid it the second you turn to the TV.

Signs: gagging, drooling, retching, belly pain.

Cooked bones →

Garlic marinade & farofa Never

Brazilian BBQ runs on garlic — in the marinade, the butter, the farofa. Garlic damages red blood cells in dogs and cats, and it's several times more potent than onion.

Signs (often delayed 1+ day): weakness, pale gums, dark urine.

Garlic →

Vinagrete (onion salsa) Never

The tomato-and-onion vinaigrette on every churrasco table is an allium double-hit: raw onion plus acidic dressing. A meat scrap dunked in vinagrete carries the risk with it.

Signs (delayed): lethargy, pale gums, reddish urine.

Onion →

Linguiça (grilled sausage) Skip it

Processed, very fatty, very salty, and usually seasoned with garlic — linguiça checks every wrong box at once. A dropped piece is a pancreatitis and allium risk in one bite.

Signs: vomiting, diarrhea, painful belly.

Sausage →

Picanha fat cap & pork cuts Careful

The famous picanha fat cap is delicious — and a fatty-meal pancreatitis trigger for pets. Same for fatty pork ribs. If you share, share lean center-cut beef only.

Signs: repeated vomiting, hunched posture, belly pain.

Pork →

The salt crust Risky

Churrasco meat is crusted in coarse salt — fine for the slice you eat, a sodium bomb for a small pet nibbling trimmings all afternoon. Cut shared pieces from the inside.

Signs: heavy thirst, vomiting, wobbliness.

Salt →
What about feijoada? Brazil's other legendary dish is a hard no for pets: it's pork and beans slow-cooked with garlic, onion and a lot of salt — the three worst offenders in one pot. Not even a spoonful of the broth.
🐱 Cats at the churrasco: a shred of plain grilled beef is fine — but cats are even more sensitive to onion and garlic than dogs, and the delayed signs are easy to miss. Keep vinagrete bowls and marinade brushes out of counter range. See foods cats should never eat.

Frequently asked questions

Can my dog have picanha?

A small piece of the lean center, plain and unseasoned, is fine for most dogs. The salt crust and the fat cap are the parts to avoid — and never the bone or skewer.

My dog stole a piece of linguiça — emergency?

One dropped piece is rarely an emergency for a healthy medium/large dog, but the fat, salt and garlic make it worth a call to a poison line, especially for small dogs or pets prone to pancreatitis. Watch for vomiting and belly pain.

Is churrasco chicken (coração/frango) ok?

Plain grilled chicken meat is fine in small amounts — but churrasco chicken is usually garlic-marinated, and chicken hearts are rich (an occasional treat at most). No bones, ever.
